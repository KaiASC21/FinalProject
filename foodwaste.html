Food Waste

Food waste is another problem we are fighting to change. Food waste targets a specific part about food loss . Food loss is the general loss of food that is being lost from it becoming spoiled or throughout any stage of it being picked in the fields to it being in someone's fridge. Food waste is our main target that we want to help reduce. Food waste is the food we humans throw away because of overproduction, bad weather, half eaten plates of food that people don't finish. Around 40% of America’s food goes to waste annually. That’s nearly around 125-160 pounds of food going to waste a year. In 2010 around 43 billion pounds of food went to waste from US retail alone. Retailers find this as a good practice to reduce the chances of overstocking in any area.

Tips for combatting Food Waste

TIP 1:

Serve yourself a reasonable amount of food that you know you can finish. Everyone sometimes overserve themselves and it's easy to happen but practice takes time!


TIP 2:

Any ingredients that haven't been used on your refrigerator, use it before it goes to waste. You can use those vegetables you haven't ate to make a salad or a healthy meal.


TIP 3:

Compost any scrap food you have left. Composting is when you use leftover foods like orange peels, banana peels, vegetable food scraps, lettuce, any organic materials. A guide to how to build a compost can be found in this link


TIP 4:

When you learn just how much you or your family really eats you can save money and buy only whats necessary.
