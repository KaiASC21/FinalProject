Food Waste

Food waste is another problem we are fighting to change. Food waste targets a specific part about food loss . Food loss is the general loss of food that is being lost from it becoming spoiled or throughout any stage of it being picked in the fields to it being in someone's fridge. Food waste is our main target that we want to help reduce. Food waste is the food we humans throw away because of overproduction, bad weather, half eaten plates of food that people don't finish. Around 40% of America’s food goes to waste annually. That’s nearly around 125-160 pounds of food going to waste a year. In 2010 around 43 billion pounds of food went to waste from US retail alone. Retailers find this as a good practice to reduce the chances of overstocking in any area.

Tips for combatting Food Waste

TIP 1, Proper portion size:

Serve yourself a reasonable amount of food that you know you can finish. Everyone sometimes overserve themselves and it's easy to happen but practice takes time! This way you are able to reduce your food waste.


TIP 2, Use everything:

Any ingredients that haven't been used in your refrigerator, use it before it goes to waste. You can use those vegetables you haven't ate to make a salad or a healthy meal. Not using the fresh produce would result in a waste of food and your money! Make sure to plan your trip to the grocery store so you know what items you don't overbuy on your trip.


TIP 3, Composting:

Compost any scrap food you have left. Composting is when you use leftover foods like orange peels, banana peels, vegetable food scraps, lettuce, any organic materials. A guide to how to build a compost can be found in this link. Compositing is also a fun activity to due in your down time. Your compost can be used in the future to grow your own plants. Create a minature garden with fresh produced. This will suyrely improv eyour diet!


TIP 4, Buy local:

Buy locally sourced food. Local foods are often fresher and require less transportation, meaning lower carbon emissions. Compared to produce found in the supermarket local produce was often packaged with less plastic packagings, and less was damaged or wasted in the packaging process. Also, it is a great idea to support local businesses!
